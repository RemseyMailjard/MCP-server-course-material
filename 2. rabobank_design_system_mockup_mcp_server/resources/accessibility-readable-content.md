# Accessibility Guidance: Readable Content

Summary: Write and structure content so people can scan, understand, and trust it quickly, even on busy pages or smaller screens.

## Guidelines
- Use headings, labels, and supporting text that say what they mean without jargon or decorative filler.
- Keep contrast strong enough for low-vision users, especially on muted text, chips, and secondary actions.
- Break longer explanations into short paragraphs or lists so information lands in manageable steps.
- Avoid using color as the only way to explain status, urgency, or selection.

## Example Prompt
Give me readability and contrast guidance for the card component and keep the advice concise.

## Joke
If users need binoculars to read the text, the design may be aiming for theatre instead of usability.
